Uyghur-Dinosaur War

The forgotten battle between nomads and ancient rulers

Prelude to Conflict

65 million years ago, dinosaurs dominated the Earth. Their civilization flourished, reaching remarkable technological heights.

When a cataclysm struck, they retreated underground, continuing their evolution in secret, hidden beneath the Earth's crust.

The Discovery of the Underground Cities

In 744 AD, the Uyghurs founded their great Khaganate, expanding across Central Asia. During an expedition, miners stumbled upon deep tunnels.

These tunnels led to vast underground cities filled with relics of an unknown, advanced civilization. The Uyghur leaders realized they had uncovered something ancient.

First Contact and Rising Tensions

In 746 AD, the Uyghurs sent an envoy into the tunnels. The dinosaurs, now a highly advanced subterranean species, saw this as an invasion.

Skirmishes broke out. The dinosaurs, wielding energy-based weaponry, easily defeated the Uyghur scouts. The war had begun.

The dinosaurs launched surprise attacks on Uyghur encampments. Their swift, reptilian warriors outmatched traditional cavalry charges.

The Uyghurs adapted, using guerrilla warfare and fire tactics to force the dinosaurs back into their underground lairs.

The Final Battle of 762 AD

At the Tola River, the war reached its peak. The Uyghur forces, led by Khagan Bayanchur, deployed thousands of warriors.

The dinosaurs countered with towering, mechanical constructs and bio-engineered creatures. The battle lasted for days.

In a desperate act, the Uyghurs triggered massive rockslides, sealing the tunnels and cutting the dinosaurs off from the surface forever.

The Aftermath

The Uyghurs celebrated their victory, but their records of the war were lost over time, turning into mere legend.

Some believe the dinosaurs still exist, hidden beneath the Earth, waiting for the right moment to reclaim their dominance.
